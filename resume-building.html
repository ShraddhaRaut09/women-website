Resume Building

Learn how to create a compelling resume that highlights your skills and experiences.

Resume Basics

Creating a strong resume is the first step toward securing your desired job. Here are some basics to get started:

  • Keep It Clear and Concise: Limit your resume to one page if possible and focus on relevant information.
  • Use Professional Language: Avoid jargon and slang. Use action words to describe your achievements.
  • Include Contact Information: Make sure your email, phone number, and LinkedIn profile (if applicable) are easily accessible.

Formatting Tips

Proper formatting makes your resume easy to read and visually appealing. Follow these tips:

  • Use a Clean Layout: Stick to a simple font, such as Arial or Times New Roman, and maintain consistent spacing.
  • Organize Sections: Divide your resume into sections like "Experience," "Education," and "Skills."
  • Highlight Key Points: Use bold or italics to emphasize job titles, company names, or achievements.

Using Keywords and Passing ATS

Many employers use an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) to scan resumes. To improve your chances:

  • Use Job-Relevant Keywords: Read job descriptions carefully and include relevant keywords in your resume.
  • Avoid Overly Complex Formatting: Keep your layout simple to ensure it is readable by ATS software.
  • Focus on Skills: Emphasize hard and soft skills that match the job requirements.
